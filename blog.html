Your experience setting up GitHub and publishing to Pages

Setting up GitHub and publishing to Pages was quite an adventure for me. Initially, I found the process
to be a bit complicated, navigating through repositories, and branches, and understanding the version
control concepts. The documentation, though comprehensive, felt overwhelming at times. Figuring out
the right repository structure and configuring the settings for GitHub Pages took some trial and error.

I encountered challenges while linking my custom domain and configuring the necessary DNS settings.
However, I gained valuable insights into the world of version control and web hosting. Despite the
initial complexity, the learning experience was invaluable, and I now feel more confident in managing
my projects on GitHub and publishing them using GitHub Pages.

Reflection on the readings you selected - relating it to your experience with the internet today

"The Library of Babel" by Jorge Luis Borges presents a fascinating reflection on the nature of
knowledge and the search for meaning in an infinite and seemingly chaotic universe of information.
In many ways, this story resonates with our contemporary experience of the internet. The internet, like
Borges' library, is a vast repository of information, offering a multitude of perspectives, ideas, and data.
We navigate through this digital labyrinth in search of knowledge, much like the inhabitants of Borges'
library.

However, just as in the story, our quest for meaning on the internet can sometimes feel overwhelming
and futile. The sheer volume of information, much of it unfiltered and unorganized, can lead to a sense
of being lost in the vastness of data. Additionally, the challenge of discerning credible sources from
misinformation mirrors the inhabitants' struggle to find meaningful books amidst the infinite possibilities
in Borges' library.

Moreover, the story touches on the human desire for order and pattern recognition in the face of apparent
chaos. In our digital age, we develop tools like search engines and algorithms to impose order on the
internet's vastness, attempting to find coherence and meaning. Yet, much like the inhabitants of Borges'
library, we often grapple with the realization that not all questions have clear answers and that understanding
everything may be an unattainable goal.

In essence, "The Library of Babel" provides a thought-provoking lens through which to consider our
relationship with the internet, prompting us to reflect on the challenges, opportunities, and inherent complexity
of navigating the boundless sea of information in our digital age.

Interesting things you have found about the history of the web

The late 1990s saw the dot-com boom, characterized by the rapid growth of internet-based companies. Many
startups emerged during this period, leading to both innovation and speculation, ultimately resulting in the
dot-com bubble burst in 2000.

Platforms like Friendster, MySpace, and later Facebook, Twitter, and LinkedIn played a significant role in
shaping the social aspect of the web. Social media transformed how people connect, share, and communicate
online.

With the widespread use of smartphones, the mobile web has become increasingly important. Responsive web
design, introduced around 2010, allowed websites to adapt to different screen sizes, optimizing the user
experience on various devices.

Reference websites

Visit the Museo Del Prado Website

The Museo del Prado's website showcases some of Spain's best fine art, with paintings spanning the entire
screen in enough detail to reveal the textures of the brush strokes. It's as if you're right up against the artwork
while watching it. Another feature I like is how each background image adjusts to the shape of the viewport.

Visit the Frans Hals Museum Website

The Frans Hals Museum exhibits both modern and 16th and 17th century art, including works by Frans Hals,
a portrait artist.Its website is possibly my favorite, with a perfect balance of animation, usability, color, and
subtle nuances on each page. I adore the elaborate scroll effects, as well as the sticky sidebar that follows you
as you scroll, ensuring that you always have access to the most crucial links.

Visit the Rijksmuseum Website

The Rijksmuseum in Amsterdam's website does not waste time in getting visitors up close and personal with
the art, allowing them to see the finer aspects of the paintings on display. They also provide a number of high-
quality video tours to help visitors better grasp the museum's offerings.
