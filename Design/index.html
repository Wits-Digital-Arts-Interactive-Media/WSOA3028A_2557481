My User Flow Diagram

Looking at my user flow diagram, you can see that users have access to every main page in my website. Having a navigation bar at the top of a website is a fundamental UI and UX choice that significantly enhances user experience and flow. Primarily, it provides users with immediate access to essential sections or pages of the website, allowing for effortless navigation. With the navigation bar consistently positioned at the top, users can quickly orient themselves and understand the structure of the site, reducing confusion and frustration. This enhances usability, especially for first-time visitors who may not be familiar with the website's layout.

Moreover, a fixed navigation bar ensures that users can easily move between pages or sections without having to scroll back to the top, saving time and effort. This seamless navigation experience contributes to a positive overall user experience, increasing engagement and satisfaction with the website. Additionally, a well-designed navigation bar with clear labels and intuitive placement fosters a sense of trust and credibility, as users perceive the website as organized and user-friendly. Therefore, incorporating a navigation bar at the top of the website is not only a good practice but also a critical element in optimizing UI and UX design.

My Colour Palette

Similarly, employing colours such as grey and beige on my website contributes to its visual appeal and user experience. Grey is often chosen for its neutral and versatile nature, serving as a backdrop that allows other colours and elements to stand out while providing a sense of sophistication and elegance. Beige, on the other hand, evokes feelings of warmth and tranquility, creating a welcoming atmosphere that encourages user engagement and exploration. Together, these colours creates a harmonious and balanced colour palette that conveys professionalism and professionalism to my website.
