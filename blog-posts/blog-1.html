Reflection on the readings you selected - relating it to your experience with the internet today

"The Library of Babel" by Jorge Luis Borges presents a fascinating reflection on the nature of knowledge and the search for meaning in an infinite and seemingly chaotic universe of information. In many ways, this story resonates with our contemporary experience of the internet. The internet, like Borges' library, is a vast repository of information, offering a multitude of perspectives, ideas, and data. We navigate through this digital labyrinth in search of knowledge, much like the inhabitants of Borges' library.

However, just as in the story, our quest for meaning on the internet can sometimes feel overwhelming and futile. The sheer volume of information, much of it unfiltered and unorganized, can lead to a sense of being lost in the vastness of data. Additionally, the challenge of discerning credible sources from misinformation mirrors the inhabitants' struggle to find meaningful books amidst the infinite possibilities in Borges' library.

Moreover, the story touches on the human desire for order and pattern recognition in the face of apparent chaos. In our digital age, we develop tools like search engines and algorithms to impose order on the internet's vastness, attempting to find coherence and meaning. Yet, much like the inhabitants of Borges' library, we often grapple with the realization that not all questions have clear answers and that understanding everything may be an unattainable goal.

In essence, "The Library of Babel" provides a thought-provoking lens through which to consider our relationship with the internet, prompting us to reflect on the challenges, opportunities, and inherent complexity of navigating the boundless sea of information in our digital age.


Interesting things you have found about the history of the web

The late 1990s saw the dot-com boom, characterized by the rapid growth of internet-based companies. Many startups emerged during this period, leading to both innovation and speculation, ultimately resulting in the dot-com bubble burst in 2000.

Platforms like Friendster, MySpace, and later Facebook, Twitter, and LinkedIn played a significant role in shaping the social aspect of the web. Social media transformed how people connect, share, and communicate online.

With the widespread use of smartphones, the mobile web has become increasingly important. Responsive web design, introduced around 2010, allowed websites to adapt to different screen sizes, optimizing the user experience on various devices.
